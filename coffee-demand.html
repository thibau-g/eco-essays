With reference to the information provided and your own knowledge, evaluate the microeconomic and macroeconomic effects of increased UK demand for branded coffee.

June 2018

A branded coffee shop is a well-established chain of retailers who sell coffee (a demand price inelastic good) and have a certain reputation with their customers. They have a high market power through their branding.
Figure one (Pie chart showing market share per brand in the UK market for branded coffee) suggests an oligopolistic market, ruled by Costa with a 31% market share, qualifying it as a legal monopoly. The market has little price competition as suggested by game theory.
Each brand has little countervailing power, no one brand can knock another out of the market as they each hold a large market share.

Coffee demand is fairly price inelastic in the UK, therefore as demand increases, the price will naturally follow. However, supply of coffee beans will also be short term demand inelastic because they must be grown annually in season. As seen on the diagram, as demand shifts right, the new equilibrium moves from P1 Q1 to P2 Q2. The increase in quantity is minimal compared to the significant price hike. As a monopoly, Costa may cope better than other brands as it benefits from economies of scale more than its competitors and may be better able to negotiate deals when demand increases.

However, the reason coffee demand is price inelastic is because it is an addictive good. Behavioural economics (Thaler) suggests that the UK public has coffee before work to get energized. We are hard-working people who spare little time during the work day. Often in a rush, commuting, consumers want to maximise utility, and hence think rationally. Instead of buying branded coffee, going out of their ways to stand in longer queues to buy an expensive beverage, they might prefer the smaller stalls, charging Â£1 a cup. The beverage is interchangeable, and the majority of it is consumed by office workers, hence they find it easier to throw a coin at the vendor selling only one product and have the coffee in an instant. I strongly believe the UK coffee demand increase is not towards branded, but rather cheap coffee.

Thibau Grumett - 15/03/2019
