Evaluate the microeconomic and macroeconomic effects of a change in a country’s exchange rate.

In the 5 years between 2007 and 2012 the real effective exchange rate of the Swiss Franc increased by 19%, the Chinese Yuan by 25%, and the Japanese Yen by 23%. However the USA, UK and Eurozone experienced a depreciation of their currencies.

Unsure

An exchange rate is defined as the amount of currency another currency can buy at a given point in time. There are three main types of exchange rates. Floating, in which it is purely the market’s demand and supply forces that determine the exchange rate. Managed, in which the government or monetary organisation of an economy will intervene to artificially change exchange rates to their desired level. Fixed, where the exchange rate is pegged to other currencies and will be bought and sold by the government or monetary organisation of the economic region in order to achieve a stable, constant exchange rate. In this example, the Swiss Franc and Japanese Yen are ‘floating’, however the Chinese Yuan or Renminbi is fixed.
